Part A: Vectors & Calculus (Q1 - Q13)
1. What is the condition for two vectors a & b to be perpendicular and to be parallel?
2. Find the angle b/w the vectors a = <2, 2, -1> & b = <5, -3, 2>. Find a × b and a vector that is orthogonal to both a & b.
3. When are 3 vectors a, b, c coplanar, i.e., they lie on the same plane? Are the vectors a = <1, 1, 2>, b = <1, -2, 1> & c = <3, -1, 5> coplanar?
4. Find a vector that is perpendicular to the plane that passes through the points P(1, 4, 6), Q(-2, 5, -1) & R(1, -1, 1).
5. If f(x,y,z) = xy/z, then find all the 1st order & 2nd order partial derivatives of f.
6. Find ∂z/∂x & ∂z/∂y when:
(i) x2 + 2y2 + 3z2 = 1
(ii) yz + x log y = z2
7. Find ∂z/∂s & ∂z/∂t when:
(i) z = x2y3, x = s cos t, y = s sin t
(ii) z = tan(u/v), u = 2s2 + 3t & v = √(s2+t3)
8. For each of a, b & c below find the following: (i) ∇f, (ii) Duf(P):
(a) f(x,y) = sin(2x+3y), P(-6, 4), u = <√3/2, -1/2>
(b) f(x,y) = y2/x, P(1, 2), u = <2/3, √5/3>
(c) f(x,y,z) = y2exyz, P(0, 1, -1), u = <3/13, 4/13, 12/13>
9. Find the points of local max, local min & saddle pts for:
(a) f(x,y) = xy + 1/x + 1/y
(b) f(x,y) = ey(y2 - x2)
10. Prove that f1 & f2 are both homogeneous & comment on their degrees, where:
f1(x,y) = x3 - 2x2y + 3xy2 + y3
f2(x,y) = x log(y/x)
11. Evaluate the following double integrals:
(i) ∫04 ∫0√y xy2 dx dy
(ii) ∫∫D y / (x5+1) dA, where D = {(x,y) ∈ R2 | 0 ≤ x ≤ 1, 0 ≤ y ≤ x2}
(iii) ∫∫D x3 dA, where D = {(x,y) ∈ R2 | 1 ≤ x ≤ e, 0 ≤ y ≤ ln x}
12. Show that any vector field of the form F(x,y,z) = <f(x), g(y), h(z)> is conservative & any vector field of the form F(x,y,z) = <f(y,z), g(x,z), h(x,y)> is solenoidal.
13. Find curl(F) & div(F) for each vector field F:
(a) F(x,y,z) = <x+yz, y+xz, z+xy>
(b) F(x,y,z) = <x/D, y/D, z/D>, where D = √(x2+y2+z2)
Part B: Matrices & Linear Algebra (Q14 - Q21)
14. Find the matrix A s.t.
(2 1 | 3 2) A (-3 2 | 5 -3) = (-2 4 | 3 -1)
15. If A = (1 1 2 | 1 9 3 | 1 4 2) & B = (1 2 0 | 2 3 -1 | 1 -1 3), then verify that (BA)-1 = A-1B-1.
16. Find the rank of the matrices:
A = (8 1 3 6 | 0 3 2 2 | -8 -1 -3 4) & B = (1 2 3 | 2 3 1 | 1 1 -2)
17. Solve the systems of linear equations:
(i) x1 + x2 + x3 = 1
x1 + 2x2 + 3x3 = 6
x1 + 3x2 + 4x3 = 6

(ii) 2x - y + 3z = 8
x - 2y - z = -4
3x + y - 4z = 0
18. When do we say that a system of linear equations is consistent? When is it inconsistent? Write the suitable conditions for determining both.
19. Test for consistency of solution(s) for the following system of linear equations and find all the solution whenever it exists:
5x + 3y + 7z = 4
3x + 26y + 2z = 9
7x + 2y + 10z = 5
20. Investigate the values of λ & μ such that the system:
2x + 3y + 5z = 9
7x + 3y - 2z = 8
2x + 3y + λz = μ
have (i) no solution, (ii) a unique solution, (iii) infinitely many solutions.
21. Verify the Cayley-Hamilton Theorem for:
(i) (1 1 3 | 1 5 1 | 3 1 1)
(ii) (1 1 3 | 1 3 -3 | -2 -4 -4)
Also find their eigenvalues & eigenvectors. Are they diagonalizable? Justify your answer. Also find their inverse whenever it exists.
Part C: Special Functions (Q22)
22. Define the Beta & the Gamma functions. Find the value of Γ(6) & β(5, 4).
Also show that β(m+1, n) = m/(m+n) β(m,n) & that β(m+1, n) + β(m, n+1) = β(m, n).
